dynamical.org

A research lab for open data about our world

dynamical.org is a not-for-profit research lab with a mission to advance humanity’s ability to access, understand, and act on accurate data about our world. Weather and climate data is our focus today; the questions we work on reach across environmental data more broadly.

We do this in two intertwined ways: we run open research into how this data should be produced, evaluated, and trusted, and we operate a public data utility that puts the results in everyone’s hands. The research sharpens the utility; operating real infrastructure at scale keeps the research honest.

Research

We treat the making and use of environmental data as open research problems — how to design a data product people can build on with confidence, how to judge which forecasts to trust for a given decision, how to know the moment data is ready and whether it can be depended upon. We work in the open and publish what we learn, in our research and updates.

A data utility

Environmental data is critical but often difficult to access, fragmented, and slow to utilize. We operate open, live-updating, cloud-optimized data infrastructure — free for anyone to use, with no lock-in — so that a researcher or engineer can load it in one line and build on it in production. Our catalog is where that work is published today.

We are not the first to publish open environmental data. What’s new is a long-term commitment to maintaining the open-source code and open data we produce, and an organization purpose-built to do just that.

Built to last

We want the data and code we produce to be stewarded by an organization that will be here for the long term. None of us want to build on a foundation that shifts or crumbles in a few years. So the organization is designed for long-term operations and stewardship using what we call "Public Benefit Corporation, Hardcore Mode." In this model we explicitly:

  1. prohibit sale of the organization;
  2. require (hypothetical) profit be reinvested in the organization's purpose; and
  3. set organizational priorities led by active members and users (for example, our committees).

The result is a stewardship organization designed to operate independently and perpetually.

Steering Committee

The dynamical.org Steering Committee has been established to guide the organization’s strategic direction and ensure the long-term alignment with our mission. The committee is comprised of distinguished users and domain experts, who will offer insights, help prioritize initiatives, and foster collaboration between stakeholders.

How They’ll Contribute:

  • Advising on project priorities.
  • Bridging the gap between open-source development and real-world applications.
  • Shaping the future of weather and climate data accessibility.

By engaging this collective expertise, the Steering Committee helps maintain the long-term relevance and integrity of our open-source tools and data repositories. Their contributions are central to our mission of fostering accessible, reliable, and actionable weather and climate data.

Notes from the Steering Committee meetings will be shared via dynamical.org's updates and newsletter.

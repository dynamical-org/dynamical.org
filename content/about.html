A not-for-profit research lab advancing how we
access, understand, and act on data about our world.

Why we exist

Prior to dynamical.org, we spent a lot of time forecasting rivers. Unsurprisingly, weather data was essential, and consumed a proportional share of the engineering work. Training global hydrology models required current forecasts, vast archives, and a custom pipeline for every source.

We saw that our peers had the same challenge so we thought, "Hey, what if we do it once, really well, for everyone."

So, we started dynamical.org around a simple idea: the world’s most useful weather and climate datasets should be as easy to open as any modern dataset (or heck, easier). Switching from a traditional forecast such as NOAA’s GFS to an AI forecast such as ECMWF’s AIFS should be trivial; building blocks to move faster on more interesting problems.

How we work

We work on two interlinked fronts:

  1. Open research
  2. A public data utility

Research

How should a dataset be designed? Which forecast should you trust for a given decision? When is a forecast actually ready? We work through these questions and more, publishing what we learn in our lab notes and updates. Sometimes this exploration results in tools or software products.

A data utility

We apply our research to an open, live-updating, cloud-optimized weather and climate catalog. It is free for anyone to use. A researcher or engineer can load a dataset in one-ish line and build on it in production. The catalog is where that work lives today.

Built to last

Open infrastructure only matters if people can trust it to stay open. We designed dynamical.org for long-term operations and stewardship using what we call "Public Benefit Corporation, Hardcore Mode." We:

  1. prohibit the sale of the organization;
  2. reinvest any profit in the organization’s purpose; and
  3. give active members and users a role in setting priorities through groups such as our steering committee.

The result is an independent stewardship organization built to outlast any one project, funder, or founder. (We may convert to a full 501c3 in the future - it's just a lot at the beginning.)

Want to support us with philanthropic dollars? Radient Earth Foundation is our fiscal sponsor. Are you a commercial entity benefiting from dynamical.org's work? Consider procuring an SLA. In either case, reach out to marshall@dynamical.org to learn more.
